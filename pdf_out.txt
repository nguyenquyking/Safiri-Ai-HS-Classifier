AI for HS Code Classification and Trade Intelligence
Overview
Accurate classification of goods using Harmonized System (HS) codes is a fundamental require-
ment in international trade. HS codes determine applicable tariffs, regulatory requirements, and
compliance obligations. Misclassification can result in shipment delays, penalties, and financial
losses.
In practice, HS code assignment is challenging because product descriptions are often ambiguous,
inconsistent, or incomplete. Different operators may classify the same product differently depending
on their interpretation.
At Safiri Ai, we are building systems that assist in intelligent, explainable HS code classification.
This assignment evaluates your ability to design such a system, focusing not only on prediction
accuracy but also on reasoning and interpretability.
Problem Statement
You are given a dataset of product descriptions along with their corresponding HS codes (at 2-digit,
4-digit, or 6-digit level).
Your task is to design and implement a system that can:
1. Predict the appropriate HS code for a given product description,
2. Provide a confidence score for the prediction,
3. Generate a clear explanation for why the selected HS code is appropriate.
Your solution should reflect how such a system could be used in a real logistics workflow.

What You Are Expected to Build
You should begin by analyzing how product descriptions map to HS codes. In many cases, de-
scriptions are noisy and lack standardization. A strong solution will account for this by applying
preprocessing, normalization, or feature extraction techniques.
You should implement a classification approach that can map product descriptions to HS codes.
This may include traditional machine learning methods, embedding-based similarity, or language
model-based approaches. The specific method is less important than your reasoning for choosing
it.
Your system should output a predicted HS code along with a confidence score. It should also
provide an explanation that justifies the prediction. For example, a useful explanation might
highlight keywords in the description or reference similarity to known examples.
You should also consider how your system handles ambiguity. In real-world settings, multiple HS
codes may appear plausible. You may choose to return top-k predictions or include uncertainty
handling, but this must be clearly explained.
Suggested Challenges to Address
You may consider addressing challenges such as:

•class imbalance across HS codes,
•overlapping semantic meaning between categories.
